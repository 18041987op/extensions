Brake Pad Replacement — Front Disc Brakes

This procedure covers the removal and installation of the front brake pads. Always follow the manufacturer's torque specifications and inspect the rotor for wear.

Removal

Raise and support the vehicle. Remove the front wheel. Remove the two caliper guide pin bolts and lift the caliper off the rotor. Suspend the caliper with a hook; do not let it hang by the brake hose.

Caution: Do not press the brake pedal while the caliper is removed.

Installation

Install the new brake pads into the caliper bracket. Compress the caliper piston using a piston tool. Reinstall the caliper and torque the guide pin bolts to spec. Pump the brake pedal to seat the pads before driving.

Torque Specifications

Caliper guide pin bolts: 26 ft-lbs. Wheel lug nuts: 80 ft-lbs. Diagnostic trouble code P0420 is unrelated to this procedure.
